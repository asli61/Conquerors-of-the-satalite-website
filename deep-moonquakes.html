Deep Moonquakes


Geologists and scientists have argued that fluid phases or partial melts in the lunar mantle could additionally play an important role, similar to the case of intermediate-depth subduction-zone seismicity on the Earth, as deep moonquakes occur at depths where brittle failure is impossible in dry rock.

Deep moonquakes occur in so-called “nests”, which repeatedly release seismic energy in specific cycles correlated with lunar tides. More than 7000 individual deep moonquakes were recorded, originating from more than 250 of these nests, which are situated at depths between 700 and 1100 km. From the limited data available, neither the spatial nor the temporal patterns of deep lunar quakes are fully understood. However, it is apparent that the activity of these nests correlates with the tidal cycles caused by the Earth and the Sun.
