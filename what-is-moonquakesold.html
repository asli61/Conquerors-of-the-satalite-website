What Is Moonquakes ?


A moonquake is the lunar equivalent of an earthquake (i.e., a quake on the Moon) although moonquakes are caused in different ways. They were first discovered by the Apollo astronauts. The largest moonquakes are much weaker than the largest earthquakes, though their shaking can last for up to an hour, due to fewer attenuating (weakening) factors to damp seismic vibrations. We know about these moonquakes with the information that comes from the seismometers placed on the moon by the Apollo missions. The instruments were placed by the Apollo 11, 12, 14, 15 and 16 missions from 1969 through 1972 and all of these instruments except the one placed by Apollo 11 -which only worked for three weeks- functioned perfectly until they were switched off in 1977.

There are four kinds of moonquakes: Deep moonquakes,Meteorite impacts, Thermal moonquakes, Shallow moonquakes
