Deep Moonquakes

The deep moonquakes are described as the process of storage and release of tidal energy but it doesn’t release any tectonic energy as the earthquakes does. There is not any tectonic seismicity observed in the moon

Deep moonquakes happen extremely often, typically on a cycle of roughly 27 days, and occur nearly 700 km below the surface of the moon These moonquakes clearly display tidal periodicities in their histories of origin times and signal amplitudes.( Physics of the Earth and Planetary Interiors, 1979)

Deep moonquakes occurring in a depth range between 700 and 1200 km (Nakamura, 2005) make up more than 55% of all identified events. (Science Direct , 2020)
