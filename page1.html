Deep moonquakes

The deep moonquakes are described as the process of storage and release of tidal energy but it doesn’t release any tectonic energy as the earthquakes does. There is not any tectonic seismicity observed in the moon

these happen extremely often, typically on a cycle of roughly 27 days, and occur about 700 km below the surface of the moon.

these occur in a depth range between 700 and 1200 km and make up more than 55% of all identified events
